Quotes and Speeches of Quaid-e-Azam

Famous Quotes by Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah:

  1. On Unity, Faith, and Discipline:

    2. "With faith, discipline and selfless devotion to duty, there is nothing worthwhile that you cannot achieve."

  2. On Pakistan’s Vision:

    3. "No nation can rise to the height of glory unless your women are side by side with you."

  3. On Democracy:

    4. "Democracy is in the blood of the Muslims, who look upon government as a sacred trust and who do not crave for personal gains by unfair means."

  4. On Religion and State:

    5. "You are free; you are free to go to your temples, you are free to go to your mosques, or to any other place of worship in this State of Pakistan. You may belong to any religion, caste, or creed that has nothing to do with the business of the State." (Speech, 11th August 1947)

  5. On Achieving Independence::

    6. "We are determined to build a country where the law and justice reign supreme, where everyone is equal, and where no one is above the law."

    Important Speeches by Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah:

  1. Speech in the Constituent Assembly of Pakistan (August 11, 1947)::

    2. "I do not believe in taking the right decision, I take a decision and make it right. You are free; you are free to go to your temples, you are free to go to your mosques, or to any other place of worship in this State of Pakistan. You may belong to any religion, caste, or creed—that has nothing to do with the business of the State." (This speech is regarded as one of his most important, setting the tone for Pakistan's commitment to religious tolerance and equality.)

  2. Speech at the All India Muslim League Session (March 22, 1940):

    3. "The Muslims are a nation by every definition. We are a nation, and we have our distinct culture, civilization, and laws. We cannot live in peace in a country dominated by another nation." (In this speech, Jinnah emphasized the need for a separate Muslim state, which later led to the formation of Pakistan.)

  3. Speech on the Day of Pakistan's Independence (August 14, 1947):

    4. "At this solemn moment in our history, we do not wish to see the bloodshed, violence, and dislocation of families that has occurred in many parts of the world. Pakistan shall not be the land of division but the land of peace and prosperity." (This was part of his inaugural address to the nation on the day of independence.)

  4. Speech at the University of Dhaka (1948):

    5. "With the creation of Pakistan, the Muslims of India have attained their rightful place in the world. But with this, comes a heavy responsibility. The great ideals of our movement are now upon our shoulders. We must rise to this occasion and show the world that we can build a great nation."

  5. Speech on the Unity of the Muslim Nation (December 25, 1947):

    6. "I am not a politician, I am a statesman. I have not sought to divide the land of the subcontinent, but to unite the Muslims in a common cause. The welfare and future of our children depend on the unity and strength of the Muslim nation."
